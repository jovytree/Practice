Discover Korea - A food lover's paradise

"All days are perfect days in Korea"
If you're a foodie, Korea is the perfect destination for you

With over 5000 years of history, Korea has developed a remarkably diverse food culture.
Korean cuisine is built around several core principles: the art of fermentation,
the use of fresh seasonal ingredients, and the balance of flavors and nutrition.
This culinary tradition has been shaped by Korea's geography, climate,
and cultural exchanges throughout its long history.

From the royal court cuisine of the Joseon Dynasty to humble street food found in bustling markets,
Korean food offers something for every palate. The concept of 'banchan' - an array of small side
dishes served with every meal - showcases the Korean philosophy that dining should be a
communal experience filled with variety and abundance. Whether you're savoring spicy tteokbokki
from a street vendor, enjoying a hearty bowl of bibimbap, or experiencing the interactive fun of Korean BBQ,
each dish tells a story of tradition, innovation, and the Korean people's deep connection to their land and seasons.

Rose Tteokbokki Recipe

Ingredients :
1 lb (450g) fresh or frozen tteok (rice cakes)
3 tbsp gochujang
2 tbsp tomato paste
1/4 cup heavy cream or milk
2 tbsp soy sauce
1 tbsp sugar
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 tbsp vegetable oil
  1. Prepare the rice cakes: If using frozen rice cakes, soak them in warm water for 10 minutes until soft.
  2. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, mix gochujang, tomato paste, soy sauce, and sugar until well combined.
  3. Cook aromatics: Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sliced onion (if using). Cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add sauce: Add the gochujang mixture to the pan and stir for 1 minute.
  5. Add liquid: Pour in 1 cup of water and bring to a boil.
  6. Cook rice cakes: Add the rice cakes and any additional ingredients like fish cakes. Cook for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and rice cakes are tender.
  7. Create the rose color: Lower the heat and slowly stir in the heavy cream until you achieve a beautiful rose color.
  8. Finish: Add green onions and cheese (if using). Cook for another 2 minutes.
  9. Serve: Garnish with sesame seeds and serve hot!
Tips:
  • Adjust the cream amount for your preferred shade of pink
  • Add more gochujang for extra spice
  • The sauce should coat the rice cakes nicely - add more water if too thick

Prep time: 10 minutes | Cook time: 15 minutes | Serves: 2-3 people
